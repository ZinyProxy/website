The Bottom Fell Out of Datacenter Proxies

Once upon a time, datacenter proxies worked just fine for scraping most sites. No more. For scraping any protected websites, they won’t work.

Here’s what happens the moment your datacenter IPs touch a modern protected website:

  • Instant blocks – Amazon, LinkedIn, Instagram throw datacenter IPs on the banned list after 20-50 requests
  • CAPTCHA hell – Anti-bot systems flag your request within a second and force you to solve endless CAPTCHAs
  • Fake data – Sites detect your requests as a bot and respond with false/incomplete data making your entire crawl pointless
  • Constant troubleshooting – You have to constantly rotate your proxies manually, troubleshoot bans, and watch your scraper repeatedly fail

Residential proxies address this fundamental problem by rotating through real household IPs from actual ISPs. To the websites you’re scraping, your traffic looks exactly like someone just browsing from their home. This legitimacy is what makes it finally possible to scrape at scale on modern websites.

Residential Proxies Explained: What are they and Why You Need Them in 2026

Residential proxies are IP addresses tied to actual residential customer addresses by Internet Service Providers. Unlike datacenter IPs that come from servers, datacenters, etc. and are obviously fake, residential IPs are tied to real households, that is why anti-bot systems have a hard time detecting them.

The Technical Detail

When websites use device fingerprinting, combining signals like your IP, user agent, browser signatures, and access patterns, residential proxies are the key to making your traffic look legitimate. They are not on any bot-tracking databases because to ISPs and network providers they’re real consumer connections.

The Practical Trade-off

Residential proxies rotate your traffic through real people’s devices (computers, phones, tablets), so they’re more expensive and a little less stable than datacenter options. Most providers charge by bandwidth usage which can be difficult to budget for in larger projects if you’re not careful.

Our Testing Methodology

In order to make the comparisons as fair as possible, we took a systematic approach for testing each of the 7 providers in our comparison:

We Scraped

  • 100,000 total page requests (broken into high-protection targets)
  • Amazon (30,000 pages): Product listings, reviews, pricing

  • Instagram (20,000 pages): Public profiles, posts, engagement data
  • Google (15,000 pages): Search results, business listings
  • Zillow (10,000 pages): Property listings across 20 cities

We Measured Success by

  • Success rate = % of requests that returned the target data without blocks, CAPTCHAs, errors
  • Speed = Avg response time from request sent to data received
  • Cost = Total spent to scrape 100,000 pages according to standard pricing tiers

Consistency Check

  • Same scraping scripts used across all providers
  • Same target pages scraped in parallel
  • Same time windows to control for day-part/site-specific fluctuations
  • Multiple test runs to ensure repeatability
