Residential, ISP and Mobile Proxies Compared

You run 50 Instagram accounts for clients. One day you check and realize 30 of them have been banned overnight. Bad luck? Wrong proxy choice.

2026 has seen the entry of actors in every market who offer proxies that are terrible and expensive or overkill for your use case. The key to succeeding long term is using proxies matched to technical capabilities to your specific requirements.

In this guide we’ll compare residential, ISP, and mobile proxies including actual pricing, performance metrics, and a decision framework for buying the right proxy type for you.

The Three Proxy Types in a Nutshell

Residential Proxies: Powered by real home internet connections

Residential proxies work by routing your requests through IP addresses assigned to physical homes by internet service providers (ISPs). When you make a request, it passes through the proxy and the destination sees a real residential user connecting from their home in Manchester, Toronto, or Tokyo.

Flow:

Your device → Proxy server → Residential user’s device → Target website

Major characteristics:

IP pool size: 10M-200M+ addresses

Geographic targeting at city level for 200+ countries

Rotation: Automatic or sticky sessions

Speed range: 2-10 Mbps (varying by end-user connections)

Trust score: High (actual residential IPs)

Residential proxies are great for the vast majority of use cases that require large IP diversity and geographic reach. However, the speed varies a lot because they go through the end-user connection which is variable.

ISP Proxies: Combining speed of datacenters with residential trust

ISP proxies (also known as static residential proxies) use IPs hosted in datacenters but registered to internet service provider autonomous system numbers (ASN). This gives you a great middle ground of datacenter infrastructure speed and the trust of residential IP registration.

Flow:

Your device → Datacenter server (with ISP ASN) → Target website

Major characteristics:

IP pool size: thousands instead of millions

Rotation: Static IPs that do not rotate unless you switch

Speed range: 50-100 Mbps (datacenter infrastructure)

Uptime: 99%+ (depends on hosting server reliability)

Trust score: High (most platforms trust these as residential)

ISP proxies are the middle ground between residential proxies and datacenter IPs in the best way possible. You give up massive IP diversity for blazing fast static IPs.

Mobile Proxies: Maximum anonymity

Mobile proxies route your traffic through mobile networks and carriers like Verizon, T-Mobile, or Vodafone. Each mobile network uses a carrier-grade NAT (CGNAT) that allows 100s-1000s of other users to share the same IP addresses. This makes it nearly impossible to track the real user.

Flow:

Your device → Mobile device/carrier network → Target website

Major characteristics:

IP pool size: Limited by carrier inventory

Rotation: Automatic but controlled by the carriers, not you

Speed range: 1-10 Mbps (depends on network quality)

Shared IPs between 100-1000+ users at a time

Trust score: Maximum (carrier IPs never get blocked)

Mobile proxies should be thought of as the big guns you use when detection leads to project failure. They are correspondingly expensive.

Proxy Performance: Speed, Cost, and Reliability Comparison

Speed and Stability

Speed is the only metric where ISP proxies win in a big way. For tasks where speed is non-negotiable like sneaker drops or scraping SERPs very quickly, ISP proxies are necessary.

Stability-wise, datacenter hosting of ISP proxies gives you much less disconnection in general compared to both residential and mobile proxies.

Cost Breakdown

Residential proxies:

Budget tier: $3-7 per GB (Ziny starts at $4/GB)

Mid-tier: $7-15 per GB

Premium: $15-30 per GB

Ideal for: If your projects use 50GB+ monthly

ISP proxies:

Budget tier: $1.50-3 per IP/month

Mid-tier: $3-8 per IP/month

Premium: $8-20 per IP/month

Ideal for: If your projects use 10-50 IPs

Mobile proxies:

Budget tier: $10-20 per GB

Mid-tier: $20-40 per GB

Premium: $40-80 per GB

Ideal for: If speed is crucial and cost is secondary

Let’s look at a real example. Scraping 100,000 pages per month can take 20-30GB of residential bandwidth (costing $60-450 with providers like Ziny) or 10-15 ISP proxies ($15-120). Mobile proxies would cost $200-600 for the same task which is fine when residential proxies are failing but not otherwise.

Detection Risk

Mobile proxies have the lowest risk because of CGNAT. When 1000s of legitimate users are sharing the same IP address, platforms risk blacklisting thousands of genuine users if they detect.

Residential proxies are next. Real IPs from real people that are very difficult to tell apart from non-proxy traffic.

ISP proxies are the rare exception that sometimes get flagged by very advanced systems that correlate the ASN numbers in public data with actual connections, but this is uncommon with good providers.

Use Cases: Which Proxy for Each Task?

Pick Residential Proxies When:

  1. Web Scraping at Scale Monitoring e-commerce prices, real estate data collection, travel site aggregation, or any project that needs 100+ unique IPs daily.

Use Case Example: A fashion retailer scrapes 500,000 products daily across 15 competitors and needs 200GB of residential bandwidth per month. The cost is $600-1,400/month. Enterprise alternative: $3,000+/month.

  1. SEO/SERP Tracking Search ranking tracking, competitor analysis, and keyword research needs geographic coverage that only residential proxies give.
  2. Ad Verification at Scale Want to check how ads display in different locations/demographics? Residential IPs allow for accurate verification.
  3. Multi-Account Management (Moderate Levels) Managing 10-50 social media accounts, marketplace seller accounts, or similar where IP diversity trumps individual IP speed.

Choose ISP Proxies When:

  1. High-Speed Scraping Scraping SERPs on Google, data-heavy APIs, or any scenario where millisecond-level response time matters. ISP proxies offer datacenter-level speeds without the datacenter reputation.

Use Case Example: An SEO firm manages 10,000 keywords on Google with 20 ISP proxies. Response time per keyword: 5 seconds. With residential proxies: 25+ seconds.

  1. E-commerce Operations Sneaker bots, event ticket purchases, and limited-release products that need speed to beat competitors.
  2. Small-Scale Multi-Account Management For 5-20 high-value accounts where consistent IP identity matters. Static nature prevents issues due to rotation.
  3. Long-Running Sessions When you need to maintain hours-long connections to the same site. ISP proxies do not randomly disconnect as residential proxies can.

Choose Mobile Proxies When:

  1. Large-Scale Social Media Management Instagram accounts in 100+ numbers, TikTok’s strict monitoring, or Facebook accounts that get blocked frequently. Mobile proxies make these tasks much more feasible.

Use Case Example: A marketing agency runs 500 Instagram accounts with mobile proxies. Monthly ban rate is <1%. With residential proxies: 15% monthly bans.

  1. Bypassing Advanced Anti-Bot Measures Sites with heavy CAPTCHA, Cloudflare challenges, or networks that block residential/datacenter IPs.
  2. Mobile App Testing App store visibility checks, mobile-only content scraping, or carrier-specific feature testing that requires genuine mobile traffic.
  3. Extreme Anonymity Requirements Operations so sensitive that any detection leads to total failure and you’re fine paying for mobile proxies.

Hybrid Approaches: Combining Proxy Types

Sophisticated operators leverage more than one proxy type.

Approach 1: Residential + ISP Mix Use residential proxies for general data discovery and scraping. Switch to ISP proxies for time-sensitive scraping or consistent identity requirements.

Savings: 40-60% lower cost than all-ISP or all-mobile.

Approach 2: Mobile for High Risk + Residential for Bulk Use mobile proxies for high-risk accounts (social media, ticket bots). Use residential proxies for scraping and research tasks.

ROI: Ideal balance, pay premium only where it prevents loss.

Approach 3: Tiered Account Management

1st Tier (Premium clients): Mobile

2nd Tier (Regular clients): ISP

3rd Tier (Testing/Low-risk): Residential

Match proxy pricing to client account value, optimize profitability.
